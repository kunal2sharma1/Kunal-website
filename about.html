About

Kunal Sharma

Research-driven analysis of power, governance, and political systems.

Background

Kunal Sharma is a postgraduate student in Political Science at the Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU), New Delhi. His academic journey is driven by a sustained curiosity about how political institutions are designed, how they function under pressure, and why they sometimes fail.

Growing up in an era of rapid political transformation — from the rise of digital authoritarianism to the resurgence of identity-based mobilisation — Kunal became drawn to the structural and theoretical questions that underpin contemporary politics. Rather than accepting surface-level explanations, his work attempts to interrogate the mechanisms of power and governance with rigour and intellectual honesty.

Academic Focus

His primary research interests span three interconnected domains:

  • Democratic Theory & Institutional Dynamics — How democratic institutions are structured, what sustains them, and what makes them vulnerable to erosion. Particular attention to India as a case study in democratic consolidation and strain.
  • Political Power & Governance — The distribution, exercise, and legitimation of power within modern states. This includes executive authority, bureaucratic politics, and the informal dimensions of governance that official frameworks often obscure.
  • Media, Communication & Political Discourse — The role of media institutions — both traditional and digital — in shaping political perception, manufacturing consent, and enabling or constraining democratic participation.

Research Approach

Kunal's approach is grounded in close reading of primary sources, comparative institutional analysis, and engagement with canonical texts in political theory. He draws on thinkers ranging from Ambedkar and Nehru to Gramsci, Foucault, and Habermas — not to cite names, but to apply frameworks that illuminate contemporary realities.

He believes that good political analysis must be empirically grounded, theoretically informed, and written accessibly — reaching beyond academic circles to those actively engaged in understanding and shaping public life.
